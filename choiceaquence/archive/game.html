Fun was never this effective

Here's how it works: Can be played with a child individually up to with a whole classroom. Using the 21 choice and 19 consequence cards, children can match each choice with a variety of possible consequences. Most excitingly played as a game show, the cards can also be placed face up for younger kids or face down as a memory game for older kids.

Ultimately, children learn how to make these connections on their own, improving their behavior, social and language skills.

Let's Choose teaches the language of social interaction by providing tools to help children practice problem solving in simulated, and eventually real, social situations. Children with autism, learning disabilities, attention deficit, and so on, can learn to be more flexible in social interactions. In addition, Let’s Choose gives them the vocabulary for more successful social interaction.

Important social skills are improved, such as:

  • Saying the person's name to get his or her attention,
  • Asking questions,
  • Answering questions,
  • Tone of voice,
  • Volume,
  • Looking at the person.

Let's Choose teaches children an intrinsic sense of responsibility. This is very different from token or reward systems which provide extrinsic reinforcement and teach children to rely too heavily on the primary adult who oversees the token system.

Let's Choose does this by providing concrete and specific examples of what the vocabulary of behavior really means. These help children learn over time that their behavior is a choice and with that there are natural consequences. As a parent, over time you also learn to identify the natural consequences in a heated moment. It allows you and other caretakers to be consistent with children.

Let's Choose works well because the game is designed with a deep understanding of the building blocks of language, a part of which is pragmatics or social skills. Understanding this changes how you as a parent talk with children and incorporate social skills into daily life.

The game provides specific vocabulary for kids to use in various social situations. It reaches the structure of asking questions and answering in grammatically correct ways. The game also helps children learn that there is often more than one outcome to a social situation, teaching flexibility.

About Let's Choose

Founded on research about language skills, social-emotional learning, and executive functioning skills, Let's Choose is a simple matching game to help kids associate choices with consequences.
