Award-winning children’s game founders announce
the launch of the interactive Super Sentence Kit

Chicago – September 29, 2011 – The founders of Let’s Choose™, an award-winning children’s matching game, announced today that they are launching the Super Sentence Kit, which assists typically developing children and those with language impairments. The Kit is the first of its kind to help students visually and kinesthetically understand, speak and write in complete sentences. It provides pre-school and school-age children a colorful experience that assists them in tackling the nebulous concept of sentence construction while helping them generate and understand sentences whether they are speaking, writing or reading.

“The Super Sentence Kit is different because it translates the confusing and tedious tasks of grammar and sentence construction into an understandable, attention-grabbing activity. The Kit is like entering the brilliant technicolor world of Oz in which students examine sentences in vivid color rather than in a previously gray world of words,” noted Elizabeth Friedman, Ph.D., Licensed Clinical Psychologist.

The Super Sentence Kit helps educators work with young children who are not yet reading or just beginning to read. Pre-school and school-aged children can use the Super Sentence Kit’s color-coded sentence card scenarios to learn the reading and writing skills that are necessary for simple, compound and complex sentence formation in an away-from-the-chalkboard learning experience.

“It is exciting when a student who started using the Super Sentence Kit at the age of four is now ‘done’ with me at age eight because she has mastered the concept of writing and speaking in full, compound sentences without repeating any words. Oral language is innate, but reading and writing are modern, learned concepts," said Stacey Buck, M.A., a Chicago speech language pathologist. Buck co-founded Let’s Choose with former teacher, Lisa Maylee, M.A. The duo’s first interactive children’s game was named Winner of The National Parenting Center's 2011 Seal of Approval.

Students using the Kit practice creating sentences with conjunctions, relative clauses and adverbials. A sixth-grade girl from Chicago whose privacy is being protected noted, “These sentence cards have helped me build detailed and interesting sentences. Now, when I get stuck in school, I can instantly picture my sentence cards and make great reports, speeches, and papers.”

Teachers appreciate the flexibility to choose sentence types based on the student’s level of comprehension and development. One catalyst for developing the Super Sentence Kit is that communication disorders are among the most common disabilities in the United States. A child's future can be improved greatly through the early identification of communication disorders and subsequent intervention. Children with disabilities, ages 3–21 in the U.S. public schools, numbered more than six million almost a decade ago. Of these children, 24% received services for speech or language disorders.1

The Kit can be used with a variety of students to facilitate complete sentence formation and, eventually, to expand these sentences.

“The Super Sentence Kit approaches the fundamentals of writing and speech through thoughtful and engaging play. Word-by-word, chunk-by-chunk, students learn to make sense of the most basic form of language: the sentence. With their teachers, students learn to recognize and appreciate interesting sentences in their reading and daily conversations. The Kit’s take-it apart, put-it-together approach engages young and curious learners,” said Amy Ludwig VanDerwater, M.A., a children’s poet and literacy consultant.

About Let’s Choose:

The Chicago-based founders of Let’s Choose, an award-winning kids matching game, are known for creating a common language around choices and consequences by using methods that reach the general education and special education of children between the ages of 3 and 18. The experiences are shared between children and parents in the home; educators in the classroom; and professionals in schools and clinic settings. Let’s Choose was created in 2002 by a teacher certified in general and special education and a speech-language pathologist. Visit Lets-Choose.com.
