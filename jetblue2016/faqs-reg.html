JetBlue Card Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What are the JetBlue Card credit offer details?

A: This offer is subject to credit approval. Not everyone will qualify for the JetBlue Card. If, at the time of your application, you do not meet the credit criteria previously established for this offer, or the income you report is insufficient based on your obligations, we may not be able to open an account for you. This offer is available to new cardmembers only. Please see Terms & Conditions for more information about rates, fees, costs and terms for this offer before applying.

Q: How do I earn TrueBlue points?

A: With your JetBlue Card, you earn 3 TrueBlue points for every $1 spent on purchases made directly with JetBlue Airways, 2 points for every $1 spent on restaurant and grocery store purchases as defined by the Merchant Category Code and 1 point for every $1 spent on all other purchases. Please note that your total purchases include purchases made by you and your authorized users, and that returns and refunds will be deducted from the total. Barclaycard is not responsible for incorrectly coded purchases.

Q: Is there a limit to the number of points I can earn?

A: No. There is no limit to the total number of points that you can earn with your JetBlue Card, as long as the program continues and your account remains open, active and in good standing

Q: How many bonus points will I earn for activating and using the card in the first 90 days?

A: The JetBlue Card will allow you to earn 5,000 bonus points after making $1,000 in purchases with your credit card account within 90 days of account opening - purchases that are not later returned or rescinded. This offer is valid for first-time cardmembers with new accounts only. Your bonus points will be deposited into your TrueBlue account 4-6 weeks after your qualifying transactions have posted.

Q: How do I redeem points for Award Flights?

A: Once you have enough TrueBlue points for an Award Flight, redeeming is easy. Visit jetblue.com/trueblue and sign in to your TrueBlue account. Click on “Book Travel.” Then, select the option to search using points.

The number of Points required for an Award Flight is determined by JetBlue in its sole discretion based on the published base fare at the time of booking, which depends on, among other things, the flight chosen and applicability of peak travel periods. The number of Points required for a particular flight may change at any time until the booking is confirmed and Points are applied as payment for the Award Flight. Taxes and fees apply to award flights, are the responsibility of the passenger, and will vary based on your departure and destination cities. See https://trueblue.jetblue.com/web/trueblue/how-it-works-using-points for full details on Award Flights.

Q: Do my TrueBlue points expire?

A: Points earned and deposited into your TrueBlue account don’t expire! However, should a member close his or her TrueBlue account, the points in that account will be forfeited. TrueBlue program Terms & Conditions apply.

Q: Are there any blackout dates for Award Flights?

A: No! Redeem for Award Flights with no blackout dates on JetBlue-operated flights.

Q: Can I save on inflight purchases?

A: Yes. You'll qualify for a 50% savings on cocktails, food and other eligible purchases. Sorry, but wireless internet access and dutyfree purchases do not qualify. Your savings will appear as a statement credit 4-6 weeks after your transaction posts to your account.

Q: What does no foreign transaction fees mean?

A: No foreign transaction fees means you don’t pay additional transaction fees on purchases you make while travelling internationally.

Q: What is a chip card?

A: In addition to storing your account information on a magnetic stripe on the back of your card, chip cards also keep your account information on a small electronic chip. Since the chip uses encryption technology, your transaction information is very secure, and your card is almost impossible to reproduce. The JetBlue Card is a chip-and-signature card with PIN capability for use at unattended terminals – offering U.S. cardmembers optimal acceptance and convenience when traveling internationally

Q: What is $0 Fraud Liability protection?

A: This means that you are not responsible for any unauthorized transactions made with your account that you report to us in a timely manner.
